FASCINATING BLACK HOLE FACTS

Event Horizon

The event horizon is the point of no return around a black hole. Once anything crosses this boundary, not even light can escape the immense gravitational pull.

Time Dilation

Time moves slower near a black hole due to extreme gravity. An observer near the event horizon would experience time differently than someone far away.

Spaghettification

Objects falling into a black hole are stretched and compressed by tidal forces, a process scientists call "spaghettification" due to the noodle-like stretching.

Supermassive Giants

At the center of most galaxies, including our Milky Way, lies a supermassive black hole millions to billions times more massive than our Sun.

Hawking Radiation

Stephen Hawking theorized that black holes emit radiation and can eventually evaporate over trillions of years, challenging the idea that nothing escapes.

Accretion Disk

Matter spiraling into a black hole forms a glowing accretion disk that can shine brighter than entire galaxies, making black holes visible from across the universe.
